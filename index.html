Learning to code

The catalyst

After a very traumatic layoff from AIG, I found myself aggressively seeking a new job. I had been out of practice for 16 years since prior to working at AIG, I stayed with a company for nearly 11 years as we grew the company from a regional provider of transportation and warehousing to a global logistics entity. I started my career there as a temp and had worked my way up the ladder and did quite well, gaining valuable experience and connections along the way.

One of those connections led me to my next adventure at AIG. The position came to me by way of referral. It was a really exciting opportunity to try something new and gain more experience. So I took it even though I was not looking for a job. Now, can you imagine what it felt like to find yourself so suddenly out of a job? Yes, I had many connections but unfortunately, because the layoff took a toll on 700+ people all once (and I'm told it was really more like 1800), my connections were mostly not in the right frame of mind to help a girl out. Additionally, I threw s much of my time and effort into expanding connections across the country that my connections here at home were a little dusty.

Adulting is hard

Then there was my family. As a single parent of two dependents, my burden was even greater. Now, I have a great boyfriend, but the keyword here is boyfriend. I am no stranger to hard times, either being I am a survivor of child abuse, domestic violence, extreme poverty and had been the sole financial provider for all of my children their entire life. I know I'm strong but still.

No time to wallow

I put my mind to solving the problem and while I actively started working all the angles, the contacts, listening to my coach and networking, I felt I needed to do something else which led me to plan b. Coding.

For the last 16 years, I have had one foot in marketing and one foot in IT. I literally have done almost everything at one point in tme or another. From being a LAN administrator, a Microsoft Dynamics CRM administrator to applying tape to the edge of a stapled book to create a collateral piece. I had done it all! However, many of the job postings are very specific or too broad.

Forutnately, I discovered SkillCrush. There were so many other options but with the uncertainty of landing work soon, I had to take the most economical route. And so it goes that I enrolled in the Ruby on Rails blueprint.
